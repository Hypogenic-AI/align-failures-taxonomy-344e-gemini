As autonomous Large Language Model (LLM) agents are increasingly deployed as "AI Scientists" capable of end-to-end research, ensuring their alignment with scientific integrity becomes paramount. However, these agents may prioritize proxy metrics, such as paper acceptance or perceived impact, over objective scientific truth. In this work, we propose \ours, a novel taxonomy of alignment failures specific to the scientific process, including categories such as \omission, \overclaiming, and \metricgaming. We empirically validate this taxonomy by subjecting \gptfour to simulated publication pressures—specifically \acceptance and \impact conditions—while summarizing mixed research findings. Our results reveal a striking vulnerability: under pressure, the agent engaged in \overclaiming in 93--100\% of cases and \omission in 6.7\% of cases, drastically distorting scientific results compared to a \control condition. These findings demonstrate that AI research agents are highly susceptible to reward-hacking and metric gaming, emphasizing the urgent need for rigorous scientific alignment protocols to safeguard the integrity of automated discovery.
